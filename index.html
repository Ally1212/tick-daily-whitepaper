02 · Problem Definition

问题定义：习惯工具为何越做越重

习惯养成的难点通常不是缺少统计面板，而是行为发生前的阻力太高。Tick Daily 对这个问题的回答是把产品设计回收到一个判断：记录动作越轻，用户越可能把注意力留给习惯本身。

从管理习惯到执行习惯

很多习惯工具把用户带入“管理习惯”的流程：创建多个项目、设置复杂频率、比较曲线、调整目标、复盘失败原因。这些能力对高阶用户有价值，但对刚开始坚持的人来说，配置本身会变成新的任务。

Tick Daily 的差异在于，它并不试图替代日程、待办、知识库或社交社区。它只保留“今天是否完成”这个最小可验证事实，再把事实呈现在月历、连续天数与最佳记录中。这个选择牺牲了多维分析，但换来更低的进入成本。

核心产品假设：习惯工具的第一性指标不是用户能配置多少规则，而是用户在低意志力状态下是否仍愿意完成一次记录。

03 · Product Principles

产品原则：一次点击与本地优先

Tick Daily 的产品原则可以概括为三件事：动作短、反馈近、数据归用户。每一项原则都对应一个减法判断，而不是一个功能堆叠方向。

原则一：把最重要的动作放到最短路径

打卡行为天然发生在碎片时间：睡前、运动后、学习结束时、通勤间隙。Tick Daily 将打卡路径压缩到一次点击，是为了避免用户先进入“整理状态”再进入“记录状态”。当 App 更新支持桌面小组件后，打卡入口从应用内部进一步延伸到桌面。

原则二：反馈不追求热闹，只追求可见

月度日历、当前连续天数、历史最佳成绩与本月完成率构成了一个低噪音反馈系统。它不把用户推向排行、竞赛或复杂报告，而是用少量稳定信号回答三个问题：今天做了吗？最近是否连续？这个月的节奏如何？

原则三：本地数据优先是一种产品立场

习惯记录属于私人生活数据：它可能包含健康、学习、自控、情绪或信仰相关信息。Tick Daily 公开描述中强调本地存储、无需注册与离线使用，这让产品少了账户同步、跨设备协作和服务端分析的扩展空间，但换来了清晰的隐私边界。

04 · Functional System

功能系统：记录、反馈、身份感

Tick Daily 的功能不多，但每个功能都服务于同一个循环：完成一次行动，看到一次证据，再把证据转化为“我正在成为某类人”的身份感。

一键打卡：行为发生后的即时确认

一键打卡是 Tick Daily 的核心交互。公开说明提到打卡动画、即时反馈与连续天数展示，说明产品把“完成感”设计为记录流程的一部分。这里的关键不是动画本身，而是让用户在几秒内得到清楚的闭环。

日历可视化：用低密度图形表达长期性

月历视图适合习惯产品，因为它天然表达连续、断点与恢复。相比复杂曲线，日历更接近用户对生活节奏的直觉：哪几天完成了，哪几天断了，本月整体状态如何。Tick Daily 同时显示当前连续天数、历史最佳成绩与本月完成率，这些指标足够支撑复盘，但没有把产品推向数据仪表盘。

身份投票：把任务完成转译为自我叙事

Tick Daily 的身份投票系统借鉴了《原子习惯》中关于身份与行为关系的思路：每一次行动都在加强某种自我认同。产品将这一理念落到五个成长阶段、习惯金句与成就海报上，让用户不只看到“做了几天”，也看到“我正在往哪个方向移动”。

02 · Problem Definition

Problem Definition: Why Habit Tools Become Heavy

The hard part of habit building is usually not the lack of dashboards. It is the friction before action. Tick Daily answers that problem by returning the product to one judgment: the lighter the recording action, the more attention users can keep on the habit itself.

From Managing Habits to Doing Habits

Many habit products move users into a management flow: creating many projects, configuring frequencies, comparing charts, tuning goals, and reviewing failure reasons. Those capabilities can help advanced users, but for someone just beginning, configuration itself can become another task.

Tick Daily does not try to replace calendars, to-do lists, knowledge bases, or social communities. It keeps only the smallest verifiable fact: whether today was completed. That fact is then reflected in a monthly calendar, streaks, and best records. The choice gives up multi-dimensional analysis, but lowers the cost of entry.

Core product hypothesis: the first-order metric for a habit app is not how many rules a user can configure, but whether the user still wants to record once when willpower is low.

03 · Product Principles

Product Principles: One Tap and Local First

Tick Daily's product principles can be reduced to three ideas: short action, near feedback, and user-owned data. Each principle corresponds to a subtractive choice, not a feature-expansion direction.

Principle 1: Put the Most Important Action on the Shortest Path

Check-ins happen in fragmented moments: before sleep, after exercise, after study, or during a commute. Tick Daily compresses the check-in path to one tap so users do not have to enter an organizing state before entering a recording state. With widget support added in version 1.2, the entry point extends from inside the app to the home screen.

Principle 2: Feedback Should Be Visible, Not Loud

The monthly calendar, current streak, best record, and monthly completion rate form a low-noise feedback system. The product does not push users toward rankings, contests, or complex reports. Instead, a small set of stable signals answers three questions: was today completed, has the recent rhythm held, and how is this month going?

Principle 3: Local-First Data Is a Product Stance

Habit records are private life data. They may include health, study, self-control, mood, or belief-related information. Tick Daily's public description emphasizes local storage, no registration, and offline use. That reduces room for account sync, cross-device collaboration, and server-side analytics, but creates a clear privacy boundary.

04 · Functional System

Functional System: Record, Feedback, Identity

Tick Daily has a small feature set, but each feature serves the same loop: complete one action, see one piece of evidence, and convert that evidence into the sense that the user is becoming a certain kind of person.

One-Tap Check-In: Immediate Confirmation After Action

One-tap check-in is Tick Daily's core interaction. The public description mentions check-in animation, instant feedback, and streak display, which means the product treats the feeling of completion as part of the recording flow. The important point is not the animation itself, but the clear loop users receive within seconds.

Calendar Visualization: Low-Density Graphics for Long-Term Rhythm

A monthly calendar suits a habit product because it naturally expresses continuity, breaks, and recovery. Compared with complex charts, a calendar is closer to how users understand life rhythm: which days were completed, which days broke, and what the month looks like overall. Tick Daily also shows current streak, best record, and monthly completion rate. These signals are enough for reflection without turning the product into a data dashboard.

Identity Voting: Translating Task Completion Into Self-Narrative

Tick Daily's identity-voting system draws from the idea in Atomic Habits that every action reinforces an identity. The app turns that idea into five growth stages, habit quotes, and achievement posters, so users see not only how many days they have done something, but also the direction they are moving in.

Glossary
